Dietary Preferences

Select your dietary preferences to get personalized recipe recommendations. Learn more about each dietary option below.

Vegan

A plant-based diet that excludes all animal products including meat, dairy, eggs, and honey.

Includes

  • Fruits and vegetables
  • Legumes and beans
  • Whole grains
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Plant-based alternatives

Excludes

  • All meat and poultry
  • Fish and seafood
  • Dairy products
  • Eggs
  • Animal-derived ingredients

Benefits

  • May reduce risk of chronic diseases
  • Lower environmental impact
  • Often higher in fiber and antioxidants

Vegetarian

A diet that excludes meat but may include dairy products and eggs.

Includes

  • Fruits and vegetables
  • Dairy products
  • Eggs
  • Grains and legumes
  • Nuts and seeds

Excludes

  • All meat and poultry
  • Fish and seafood
  • Animal-derived rennet

Benefits

  • Lower saturated fat intake
  • May support heart health
  • More sustainable than meat-based diets

Gluten-Free

A diet that excludes gluten, a protein found in wheat, barley, rye, and triticale.

Includes

  • Fruits and vegetables
  • Meat and poultry
  • Fish and seafood
  • Rice and quinoa
  • Gluten-free grains

Excludes

  • Wheat and wheat products
  • Barley and rye
  • Most conventional breads
  • Many processed foods

Benefits

  • Essential for those with celiac disease
  • May reduce inflammation
  • Can help with gluten sensitivity symptoms

Dairy-Free

A diet that excludes milk and milk products from cows, goats, and sheep.

Includes

  • Plant-based milks (almond, soy, oat)
  • Dairy-free cheeses
  • Coconut yogurt
  • All fruits and vegetables
  • Meat and fish

Excludes

  • Milk (whole, skim, etc.)
  • Cheese
  • Yogurt
  • Butter
  • Ice cream

Benefits

  • Essential for lactose intolerance
  • May improve skin conditions
  • Can reduce digestive discomfort

Nut-Free

A diet that excludes all tree nuts and peanuts to prevent allergic reactions.

Includes

  • Seeds (sunflower, pumpkin)
  • Seed butters
  • All fruits and vegetables
  • Grains and legumes (except peanuts)
  • Meat and dairy

Excludes

  • Peanuts
  • Tree nuts (almonds, walnuts, etc.)
  • Nut butters
  • Foods processed with nuts

Benefits

  • Essential for nut allergies
  • Prevents allergic reactions
  • Safer for nut-free environments

Keto

A high-fat, adequate-protein, low-carbohydrate diet that forces the body to burn fats rather than carbohydrates.

Includes

  • Meat and poultry
  • Fish and seafood
  • Eggs
  • Low-carb vegetables
  • Healthy fats

Excludes

  • Grains and starches
  • Sugary foods
  • Most fruits
  • Legumes
  • Root vegetables

Benefits

  • May promote weight loss
  • Can improve blood sugar control
  • May reduce seizures in epilepsy
